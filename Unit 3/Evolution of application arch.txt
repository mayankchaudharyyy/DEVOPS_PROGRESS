Evolution of Application Architecture

Application deployment has evolved through three major phases:

1. Physical Servers (1980s–1990s)
- Monolithic applications
- Physical data center servers
- Waterfall development
- High cost and difficult scaling

2. Virtualization Era (2000s)
- Virtual machines on shared hardware
- N-tier architecture
- Agile development
- Better resource utilization and scalability

3. Cloud-Native Era (2010–Present)
- Microservices and containers
- Cloud platforms and Kubernetes
- DevOps and CI/CD
- Auto-scaling, faster deployments, and lower costs

Key Takeaway:
Application architecture has shifted from hardware-focused systems to cloud-native, software-driven solutions that are faster, scalable, and easier to manage.
